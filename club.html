What it is — The ETH Robotics Club brings together 300+ students from across ETH Zürich to build real robots, replicate frontier research, and do things that student teams aren't supposed to be able to do. With 1M+ CHF in funding, we run a fully-equipped lab and operate a fleet of cutting-edge platforms.
01

What we do

We give students access to robots they'd otherwise only read about in papers — and we actually build things with them. Projects span the full robotics stack: hardware design and fabrication, firmware, perception, planning, control, and learning.

Active work spans bimanual manipulation, legged locomotion, dexterous grasping, and training Video-Action models on our own data. Every project is student-owned, end-to-end.

Robot Learning Division

Led by Alejo Restrepo

25 students. Real robots. Real models.

The Robot Learning division builds new robotic setups from scratch, collects teleoperated demonstration data, and trains imitation learning and video-action policies — targeting long-horizon dexterous manipulation tasks that are genuinely hard.

  • Building and iterating on hardware platforms
  • Collecting high-quality teleoperated demo datasets
  • Training and finetuning imitation learning policies
  • Pushing toward Video-Action models on our own data

Our first project — finetuning ACT for autonomous towel folding on a custom bimanual setup — shipped at the end of the 2025 semester. The next platform is a half-humanoid setup with dexterous hands, currently in development.
